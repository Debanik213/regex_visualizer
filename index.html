  • ∪
    Union: If R, S are REs then R | S represents L(R) ∪ L(S).
  • ·
    Concatenation: If R, S are REs then RS represents L(R)L(S).

Chapter Four

Kleene's Theorem

"A language is regular if and only if it is recognized by a finite automaton."

This theorem establishes the functional equivalence between Regular Expressions and Finite Automata. It allows conversion between representations without losing expressive capability.

05
Chapter Five

Mathematics of
Equivalence Verification

A rigorous four-step pipeline to prove two Regular Expressions define the same language:

01

Thompson's Construction

Syntactically parsing the RE to build an NFA with ε-transitions. Each RE operator maps to a specific structural template.

02

Subset Construction

Converting the NFA into a DFA by computing ε-closures. Eliminates nondeterminism by representing NFA state sets as single DFA states.

03

Hopcroft's Algorithm

Minimizing the DFA to canonical form. Partitions states into equivalence classes, merging indistinguishable ones.

04

Structural Isomorphism

Comparing two minimal DFAs. If a bijection exists between their states and transitions, the expressions are equivalent.
